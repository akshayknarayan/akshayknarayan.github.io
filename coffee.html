Coffeeshop Recommendations

I like coffee, especially cappuccinos. My friend Justine has a fantastic list of good coffeeshops in a few places around the world; read her list too.

Note: When it comes to coffee, I mostly evaluate cappuccinos.

Boston/Cambridge

  • George Howell Coffee is the best Boston cappuccino I've had thus far. The flavor is well balanced, and the baristas pour excellently.
  • 1369 Coffee, Crema Cafe, and Barismo are good options in Cambridge. Crema uses George Howell beans and is also a good lunch spot.
  • Area Four has the best cappuccino near the Stata Center.
  • Flat Black, near Downtown, is operated by Australians and thus has flat whites; Pavement is a good option if you're in Back Bay.
  • Kung Fu Tea is a boba chain with a few locations in the Boston area. It's not as good as Asha (see below), but it's decent. Tealuxe's boba is not my favorite, but they make a decent masala chai. They also have a good loose-leaf selection.

SF/Berkeley

  • Ritual Coffee Roasters is probably my favorite cappuccino in San Francisco.
  • Dandelion Chocolate may have chocolate in its name, but they serve great mochas! I recommend the Mission Hot Chocolate or Mission Mocha (they use Four Barrel beans).
  • Philz is good at non-espresso coffee. There are several locations, including in the South Bay.
  • Asha is, as far as I'm concerned, the One True Boba Place.

South Bay

  • Chromatic, at Lawrence and Stevens Creek (Cupertino), has flat whites.
  • Cafe Venetia is my favorite in Palo Alto.
  • Red Rock Coffee is my favorite in Mountain View.
